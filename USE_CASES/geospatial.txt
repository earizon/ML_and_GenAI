## geo maps processing

Summary from Maryam Miradi post @ LinkedIn.

- Remote sensing images are data-rich snapshots of the Earth’s surface 
- captured by sensors on satellites, drones, and aircraft.

These sensors detect electromagnetic signals in various wavelengths, including 
visible light, infrared, and radar.

- Data is captured passively (e.g., sunlight reflection) or actively (e.g., radar pulses). 
- High-resolution imagery reveals detailed spatial patterns. 
- Multispectral data captures environmental details invisible to the naked eye.

### Applications

- Disaster response: Monitor floods, wildfires, and earthquakes in near-real-time.
- Urban    planning: Analyze land use and infrastructure growth.
- Agriculture      : Assess crop health and predict yields. 


these images require significant expertise to process that can proffit from VLMs and LLMs
to automate workflows:

A Comparison of State-of-the-Art Models: [[doc_has.comparative]]

### GeoChat
-  Key Feature: Conversational querying for geospatial data.
-  Benefit: Enables non-experts to extract insights with natural language prompts.
-  Limitation: Computationally intensive for ultra-high-resolution data.

### GEOBench-VLM
- Key Feature: Benchmarking for geospatial VLM performance. 
- Benefit: Standardized metrics ensure robust model evaluation. 
- Limitation: Dataset availability may restrict broader applicability. 

### RS5M Dataset
- Key Feature: A massive dataset of image-text pairs.
- Benefit: Fine-tunes models for domain-specific tasks like disaster monitoring. 
- Limitation: High-quality labeling is resource-intensive. 

### VHM 
- Key Feature: High interpretability for sensitive applications. 
- Benefit: Builds trust in AI systems for disaster response or policymaking. 
- Limitation: Limited scalability for unstructured data. 

### EarthGPT 
- Key Feature: Multimodal analysis combining multisensor data. 
- Benefit: Integrates diverse datasets for richer insights. 
- Limitation: Complex implementation hinders adoption for smaller teams.
