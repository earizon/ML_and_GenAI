## MLOps Kubeflow vs Dagster vs ...

By Lucas Gonthier, founding machine learning engineer @Kawaak

Choosing the wrong ML orchestrator can blow up the budget and complexity. Here's how to choose, from the simplest to the most complex:

### Level 0: bash + cron script, (No orchestrator)

- Just a cron that launches a .sh. If the pipeline is 3 linear stages, that is 
  enough. Many people skip this step out of ego. [[mlops.101]]

- Bonus level 0: GitHub Actions A cron in the cloud, free (2,000 minutes/month 
  on the free tier). Perfect for a lightweight pipeline with no infrastructure to 
  maintain. You push, it turns. Zero setup. [[PM.price.free_tier]]


### Level 1: Prefect / Dagster:

- You want scheduling, monitoring, retries.
- Your pipeline has 5 to 10 stages with dependencies.
- Quick setup. Clean UI.
- Perfect for a small team.

### Level 2: Airflow

- You manage dozens of DAGs.
- Several teams.
- Cross dependencies.
- Airflow is ugly, verbose, sometimes painful. But it scales. And everyone knows him.

### Level 3:
- Kubeflow You are on Kubernetes.
- Your pipelines are heavy.
- You need container isolation, native versioning, GPU scheduling.
- High installation complexity. But maximum power.

> The rule is simple: If you can solve your problem with the level below, do it.
> Over-engineering an orchestrator costs more than the wrong model in production.
> The best orchestrator is the one a team understands in 10 minutes.
