## Yoshua Bengio: (Turing Price 2018).  Founder and director of the Quebec Institut "Mila".

(C&P from Damien Benveniste) 

... one of the "Godfathers of Deep Learning"! 

* 1994 - Identified the problem of vanishing and exploding gradients in RNN.
  <https://lnkd.in/gNNWDnGG>
* 1995 - He applied with Lecun Convolutional Neural Network to the speech and
  time series learning task: <https://lnkd.in/gSJT7rd4>
* 1998 - He suggest with LeCun a new CNN architecture (LeNet - Graph Transformer
  Networks) for the document recognition learning task:
  <https://lnkd.in/gFyijKin>
* 2003 - Proposed the first neural language model
  <https://lnkd.in/gJgngk_K>
* 2006 - Proposed a new greedy training method for Deep Networks.
  <https://lnkd.in/g4TfHwKc>
* 2008 Invented the Denoising Autoencoder model.
  <https://lnkd.in/gUvwXGNN>
* 2009 Developed a new method called curriculum learning, which is a
  form of structured learning that gradually exposes models to more
  difficult examples: <https://lnkd.in/gyBirMxN>
  [[training.curriculum_learning]]
* 2010 Proposed new unsupervised learning technique,
  "Stacked Denoising Autoencoder architecture" 
  <https://lnkd.in/gyH5-JTs> [[architecture.Stacked_Denoising_Autoencoder,training]]
* 2010 Proposed with Glorot the weight initialization technique used in
  most modern neural nets.  <https://lnkd.in/g4nqvxzh>  [[training.101,training.Glorot]]
* 2011 Invented the ReLU activation function: 
  <https://lnkd.in/gwJsxHYQ>  [[training.ReLU]]
* 2011 - Proposed Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter optimization:
  <https://lnkd.in/geSTQZWU> [[training.tunning,training.hyperparameter]]
* 2012 - Proposed the random search technique for hyperparameter optimization:
  <https://lnkd.in/gtx_ABwi> [[training.tunning,training.hyperparameter]]
* 2013 - Proposed a unifying perspective on representation learning:
  <https://lnkd.in/gVFU7iUh>
* 2013 - Invented the gradient clipping strategy to prevent the vanishing and
  exploding gradient problem in RNN.
  <https://lnkd.in/gQWkKMYq> [[architecture.RNN,training.tunning]]
* 2013 - Proposed a new activation function for Maxout networks:
  <https://lnkd.in/gWdB72dH> [[PM.TODO]]
* 2014 - Proposed the RNN encoder-decoder architecture:
  <https://lnkd.in/gnGFsdJe>
* 2014 - Proposed the gated recursive convolutional method for ML translation.
  <https://lnkd.in/g-rRQ6km>
* 2014 - He invented the Attention mechanism with Bahdanau:
  <https://lnkd.in/g2C3sHjf>
* 2014 - Proposed FitNets, a new distillation method to compress Deep NN:
  <https://lnkd.in/gsQy8f8m> [[scalability.distillation]]
* 2014 - Showed the efficacy of transfer learning:
  <https://lnkd.in/g4vfNNUx> [[PM.TODO]]
* 2015 - Proposed BinaryConnect, a new method for efficient training with 
  binary weights: <https://lnkd.in/g-Pg63RT>
* 2015 - Proposed a visual Attention mechanism for image caption generation:
  <https://lnkd.in/gbmGDwA2->
* 2017 - Proposed a novel architecture with self-attention layers for
  graph-structured data: <https://lnkd.in/gg8tQBtP>  [[data.graph]]
* 2017 - Proposed new convolutional architecture for brain tumor segmentation:
  <https://lnkd.in/gxFuqr53By>
... the way, you should read his book: <https://lnkd.in/dW2mYzxg!>
* is author also of <http://www.deeplearningbook.org/>

## Geoffrey Hinton (Nobel Price in Physics)

* invented with two partners the retroprogramming algorithm core in modern techniques of 
  neural network programming.
* 2009: developes Neu.Net. for voice recognition much better that anything 
  existing at that moment. 3 years later probed Neu.Nets could recognise images with better 
  precission than any other current technology.

* Yann LeCun made important contributions to the retroprogramming algorithms created by Geoffrey Hilton.
  Before that, in 1989, he created LeNet-5m a well-known system for recognition of written 
  characters in bank checks that at the time represented a great advance in optical character 
  recognition.
