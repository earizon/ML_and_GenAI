# Prompt "Engineering"

## Prompting 101

 Contrary to a Search engine where we place a text or question waiting for an answer,
in an LLM prompt we must provide **context** to avoid potential allucinations.

 The context is what really helps the underlying GPT architecture to find for the
desired related information and skip non-related information.

  When appropiate, do not ask for a single response, ask for as much as your prompting
budget allows you.

## "Explain your reasoning step by step before answering" 8 words "Miracle"

Initial prompt engineering started to grew in complexity:

- People traded massive prompt templates.
- Threads promised "perfect prompts."

As a consecuence:

- The prompts got longer and rigid.
- The results got more predictable.
- Models "played it safe" instead of exploring ideas.

Then a group of researchers connected to Stanford They noticed that 

"""Large language models don’t just autocomplete text.
   **They simulate reasoning when invited to do so**.""" [[prompting.101,doc_has.keypoint]]

- When prompts demanded final answers immediately, models collapsed their internal reasoning into a short path.
- But when prompts encouraged process, performance jumped:
  - More original ideas.
  - Fewer shallow answers.
  - Better logical consistency.
  - Higher creativity scores.


### "Explain your reasoning step by step before answering"

- Eight words.
- No frameworks.
- No role-playing.
- No giant prompt blocks.
- No fine-tuning.
- No plugins.
- No custom training.


- Just an instruction that changes how the model allocates attention.

When you ask for reasoning before answers, three things happen inside the model:

1. The model slows down: 
  - Instead of jumping to the most statistically likely response, it explores intermediate paths.
  - Creativity lives in those paths:

2. Multiple solution branches appear:
  - Step-by-step reasoning encourages the model to surface alternatives, trade-offs, and edge cases.

3. The model self-corrects
  - **As it reasons, it notices weak assumptions and adjusts**,
    often producing better final outputs than direct prompting.

Just better instructions.


**The future belongs to people who understand how models think, not those who memorize prompt formulas**.

## Planning and decision making

Smart planning and decision making are critical to achieving predefined goals.

As they are trained on vast amount of world knowledge and human examples, LLMs
are known to have already absorbed rich commonsense that makes it possible
to propose reasonable plans conditioned on problem setting and environmental states 
(Huang et al., 2022a; Zhang et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023b; Huang et al., 2022b; Wang et al., 2023a; Yao et al., 
2022; Yang et al., 2023).

# Prompt-Engineering @ OpenAI

* <https://platform.openai.com/docs/guides/prompt-engineering>

Open AI just released their own Prompt Engineering guide. 

... summary of their 6 strategies for getting better results when prompting GPT:

1. Write Clear Instructions:
  - Be specific: Clarity in instructions leads to more relevant outcomes.
  - Define the desired output length and complexity.
  - Demonstrate preferred formats.
  - Minimize ambiguity to enhance model accuracy.
2. Provide Reference Text:
  - Counteract potential fabrications with concrete reference materials.
  - Reference texts guide the model towards accurate and reliable answers.
3. Split Complex Tasks into Simpler Subtasks:
   - Break down tasks to reduce errors and improve manageability.
   - Consider tasks as workflows of simpler, interconnected steps.
4. Give the Model Time to "Think"
   - Allow the model to process and reason, similar to a human solving a complex problem.
   - Encourage a "chain of thought" approach for more accurate reasoning.
5. Use External Tools
   - Supplement the model's capabilities with specialized tools for specific tasks.
   - Leverage resources like text retrieval systems or code execution engines.
6. Test Changes Systematically
   - Measure improvements with a comprehensive testing approach
   - Ensure that modifications lead to overall performance enhancements.
