## "Explain your reasoning step by step before answering" 8 words "Miracle"

  A group of researchers in Stanford noticed next important fact.

> Large language models don’t just autocomplete text.
>   **They emulate reasoning when invited to do so**.
[[prompting.101,doc_has.keypoint]]

- When prompts demanded final answers immediately, models collapsed their internal reasoning into a short path.
- But when prompts encouraged a thinking process, the quality of the answer just jumped providing:
  - More original ideas.
  - Fewer shallow answers.
  - Better logical consistency.
  - Higher creativity scores.

The "Explain your reasoning step by step before answering" excels because:
- It does not require new frameworks, reusing the LLM in place.
- It does not require new role-playing.
- It does not require new giant prompt blocks.
- It does not require new fine-tuning of the internal Neural Network (costly and requiring lot of expertise).

It is just an prompt instruction that changes how the model allocates attention.

When you ask for reasoning before answers, three things happen inside the model:

1. The model slows down: 
  - Instead of jumping to the most statistically likely response, it explores intermediate paths.
  - Creativity lives in those paths:

2. Multiple solution branches appear:
  - Step-by-step reasoning encourages the model to surface alternatives, trade-offs, and edge cases.

3. The model self-corrects
  - **As it reasons, it notices weak assumptions and adjusts**,
    often producing better final outputs than direct prompting.

Just better instructions.

**The future belongs to people who understand how models think, not those who memorize prompt formulas**.


## think-step-by-step vs fine tunning


* LLMs are trained in "epochs" until the reach an stable behaviour and are able to guess data with confidence.
* To do so, they have been pre-trained on huge (terabytes) amounts of text data. This allows the LLM to
- understand language structure and language thinking structures (in different humand languages).
- Set phrases.
- General knowledge.
- ....
* Fine tunning continues the training with new "epochs" (and some Neural Networks advanced techniques) training
  in a particular set of domain-scoped data (health, finance, physics, computer programming, ...).
  During the fine-tunning the Neural Network "forgets" non important data and strengthen its behaviour for
  the particular area or domain of interest.
* Said otherwise, fine tunning is a technique to re-traing a given LLM neural network to provide scoped and focused 
  answer in a particular context or area of knowledge.
* In practice it requires to come back to the "workbench" and call LLM developers to setup "complex" frameworks
  (PyTorch, ...) and hardware (powerful GPUs) to continue the evolution of a generic (base) mode".
  (This is not exactly true anymore as of 2026 since the process has been mostly automated and now is possible to
   fine tune base models from OpenAI, Mistral, ... using a web portal and paying for the service).

* In some contexts, specially AI agents, it the "fine tunning" of prompts is mentioned. This fine tunning of
  prompts has nothing to do with the fine-tunning of the LLM:
  - prompt fine-tunning does not require to re-traing the LLM model.
  - prompt fine-tunning just adjust the prompt to obtain better results.
  - LLM    fine-tunning generated a brand new model that will perform better for the target domain in most/all
    circumstances, even with a "low quality" prompt. It will underperform in any other domain not targeted during
    the fine-tunne "post training".
