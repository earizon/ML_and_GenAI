## Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks  (KANs). Alternative to perceptrons

* <https://github.com/mintisan/awesome-kan>
* <https://www.infoq.com/news/2024/08/interpretable-kan/>

By Andreas Schlueter, Sr. AI Engineer <https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7191628418973650944/>

* KANS is a new alternative to Multilayer Perceptron MLP ... KANs use no linear 
  weights and train the activation functions instead!!!.  This allows the model 
  to use several orders of magnitudes less trainable parameters while achieving 
  extraordinary results.  [[scalability.KAN]]

- The learned activation functions are surprisingly interpretable. [[qa.interpretability]]


comparing performance of KANs over traditional MLP modeling 
several problems in physics and mathematics they found that
KANs achieved better accuracy with fewer parameters:
- **in some cases, 100x accuracy with 100x fewer parameters**. 
- The researchers also showed that visualizing the KAN's activation functions 
  helped users discover symbolic formulas that could represent the physical 
  process being modeled. [[qa.interpretability.101]]

## KANs "BUTs" by Daniel A.

* <https://www.linkedin.com/posts/daniel-aronovich_neuralnetworks-science-machinelearning-activity-7191699872700383232-nBJm>


* KANs are centered on the development and analysis of a new neural network 
  architecture inspired by the Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem.

After reading the paper, my understanding of the limitations are:

1. KANs are fundamentally more complex to implement. 
2. Although KANs are parameter efficient, the individual parameters (learnable functions on each edge)
   might require more complex and potentially computationally expensive operations, 
   such as evaluating and updating spline functions during training.
3. KANs might face scalability issues, particularly in high-dimensional settings. 
4. Issues with training stability and convergence due to the complex nature of the learnable functions
5. While one of the advantages of KANs is their interpretability, the intricate structure of having
   multiple learnable functions can also obscure understanding.
6. KANs are heavily reliant on the Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem, which might limit their
   applicability to types of data or problems where this theoretical framework is not ideal.
7. The shift from conventional network architectures to KANs might require significant changes in the way practitioners design, train, and deploy neural networks. This could slow the adoption of KANs in the wider community.

This doesn't mean that we should ignore this idea; on the contrary, let's dive deeper.

But as the saying goes,
